What is Origami?

Origami is the ancient Japanese art of paper folding. It involves creating intricate and often delicate sculptures using just a single sheet of paper, meticulously folded into various shapes and designs without the use of scissors or adhesive. The art of origami showcases the beauty of simplicity and precision, with a focus on transforming a flat piece of paper into three-dimensional works of art.

Quick Origami History

So who invented origami? It is impossible to tell. What history can tell us, is that the strongest and longest origins of origami are found in Japan. In Japan, historians have found evidence of origami dating back to 1680, through the writings of Ihara Saikaku. Ihara Saikaku detailed the role of origami in Japanese culture in his stories and poems, as origami found its way into the traditions of weddings, gift-giving and other practices. There are also many traditional Japanese stories that feature origami, such as Abe no Seimei. Just over one hundred years later, in 1797, Akisato Rito published the first origami book, Hiden Senbazuru Orikata, The Secret to Folding Cranes.
